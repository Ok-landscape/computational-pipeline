**Title:** Simulating the R√∂ssler Attractor in Python: A Visual Introduction to Chaos Theory

**Body:**

I created a notebook exploring the R√∂ssler attractor‚Äîa beautiful example of deterministic chaos that's simpler than the famous Lorenz system.

**What is it?**

The R√∂ssler system is three coupled differential equations:
- dx/dt = -y - z
- dy/dt = x + ay
- dz/dt = b + z(x - c)

Think of it as a mathematical recipe where each variable's rate of change depends on the others.

**ELI5 Version:**

Imagine spinning a ball on a string, but occasionally giving it a "kick" (the z equation). The ball traces a spiral pattern but never quite repeats its path‚Äîthat's chaos!

**The "Butterfly Effect" Demo:**

I ran two simulations with initial conditions differing by just 0.000001. At first they're identical, but after ~30 time units, they've completely diverged. This exponential separation is quantified by the Lyapunov exponent (Œª‚ÇÅ ‚âà 0.07).

**Code Stack:**
- numpy for arrays
- scipy.integrate.odeint for solving ODEs
- matplotlib for 3D visualization

The notebook includes:
- 3D attractor visualization
- xy, xz projections
- Time series plots
- Lyapunov exponent estimation
