Title: Visualizing Covering Spaces: How the Real Line Wraps Around a Circle

Body:

I created some visualizations to understand covering spaces in algebraic topology, and wanted to share the key insights.

**What's a covering space?**

Imagine wrapping a line around a circle like a helix. The projection p: R → S1 given by p(t) = (cos(2πt), sin(2πt)) is the "universal covering" of the circle. Every integer point on R maps to the same point on S1 - this set is called the "fiber."

**Why does this matter?**

There's a beautiful correspondence theorem: covering spaces of X are classified by subgroups of π1(X) (the fundamental group). For the circle, π1(S1) = Z, so:
- The universal cover R corresponds to the trivial subgroup {0}
- The n-fold cover (circle wrapping n times) corresponds to nZ

The degree of the covering equals the index of the subgroup!

**Deck transformations**

These are symmetries of the covering space that preserve the projection. For R → S1, they're just translations by integers: τn(t) = t + n. The group of deck transformations is isomorphic to π1 of the base!

**Visualizations include:**
- 3D helix showing R covering S1
- n-fold coverings for n = 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 8
- R2 covering the torus with fundamental domains
- Deck transformation action on fibers
