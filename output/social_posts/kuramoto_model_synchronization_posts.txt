The governing equation:
dθᵢ/dt = ωᵢ + (K/N)∑ⱼsin(θⱼ - θᵢ)

Observed the synchronization phase transition at K_c = 2γ = 1.0. Order parameter r exhibits mean-field behavior, with steady-state scaling as r ≈ √((K - K_c)/K) for K > K_c.

### Reddit (r/learnpython or r/physics)

**Title:** I simulated the Kuramoto model to understand how oscillators synchronize - here's what I learned

**Body:**

**The Question:** How do independent oscillators (like neurons, fireflies, or power generators) spontaneously synchronize?

**The Model:**

The Kuramoto model describes N oscillators, each with its own natural frequency ωᵢ. They're coupled through sine interactions:

dθᵢ/dt = ωᵢ + (K/N)∑ⱼsin(θⱼ - θᵢ)

The coupling term pulls each oscillator toward the others. When coupling strength K is weak, everyone does their own thing. But increase K past a critical value K_c, and order emerges!

**Key Findings:**

1. **Phase transition exists** - Below K_c, r ≈ 0 (no sync). Above K_c, synchronization emerges suddenly.

2. **Partial sync is normal** - Even with strong coupling, oscillators with extreme frequencies can't lock to the group. You get r ≈ 0.8, not perfect r = 1.

3. **Mean-field magic** - Oscillators don't "see" each other directly; they couple to the collective rhythm.

**The Code:**

Used NumPy + SciPy's odeint for integration. The trick is computing pairwise phase differences efficiently with broadcasting.

**Real applications:** neural synchronization, cardiac pacemakers, power grid stability, chemical oscillators.

I recently completed a computational investigation of the Kuramoto model, a foundational framework in nonlinear dynamics that describes how coupled oscillators achieve synchronization.

**Technical Approach:**
- Simulated N=100 phase oscillators with Lorentzian-distributed natural frequencies
- Solved coupled ODEs using SciPy's odeint with efficient NumPy broadcasting
- Analyzed the order parameter r across varying coupling strengths K

**Key Results:**
The system exhibits a clear phase transition at K_c = 2γ. Below this threshold, oscillators remain incoherent (r ≈ 0). Above it, collective synchronization emerges with the steady-state order parameter following mean-field predictions.
