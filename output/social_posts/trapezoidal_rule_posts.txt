TITLE: Understanding the Trapezoidal Rule - A Visual Python Implementation with Convergence Analysis

BODY:

**What is it?**

The Trapezoidal Rule is a numerical integration technique that approximates the definite integral of a function by fitting trapezoids under the curve. It's one of the simplest Newton-Cotes formulas and a great starting point for numerical analysis.

**How it works (ELI5):**

Imagine you want to find the area under a curvy line. Instead of doing complex calculus, you draw straight lines connecting points on the curve. Each section becomes a trapezoid (like a rectangle with a slanted top). Add up all the trapezoid areas, and you get a pretty good estimate!

**The Math:**

The composite formula is:
∫ₐᵇf(x)dx ≈ h[(f(a)+f(b))/2 + Σf(xᵢ)]

where h = (b-a)/n is the step size.

**Why it matters:**

- Error is O(h²) - doubling your points quarters your error
- Stable and works for any continuous function
- Foundation for understanding more advanced methods

**What I learned:**

1. Richardson extrapolation can estimate error without knowing the exact answer
2. The error ratio of ~4 when halving h beautifully confirms the O(h²) theory
3. Even simple methods need many subintervals for transcendental functions like exp(-x²)
