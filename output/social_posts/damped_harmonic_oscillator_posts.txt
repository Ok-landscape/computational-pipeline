Computational Physics: Understanding Damped Harmonic Oscillators

I've published a new Jupyter notebook exploring one of the most fundamental models in physics and engineering - the damped harmonic oscillator.

KEY HIGHLIGHTS:

Mathematical Foundation:
The system follows the differential equation m*d2x/dt2 + c*dx/dt + k*x = 0, which describes everything from mechanical springs to electrical RLC circuits.

Three Damping Regimes Explored:
- Underdamped (gamma < omega_0): Oscillatory decay - seen in musical instruments
- Critically damped (gamma = omega_0): Fastest return without overshoot - optimal for shock absorbers
- Overdamped (gamma > omega_0): Slow exponential return - highly viscous systems

Technical Approach:
- Used scipy.integrate.odeint for numerical integration
- Validated against analytical solutions (error ~ 10^-9)
- Computed phase space trajectories and energy dissipation curves
- Calculated Quality Factor Q = omega_0/(2*gamma) for oscillator characterization

The Quality Factor quantifies energy storage vs. dissipation - a critical parameter in resonance design, from radio circuits to quantum systems.

This analysis forms the foundation for understanding resonance phenomena, mechanical engineering design, and even quantum mechanics.
