**Title:** Built a Conway's Game of Life simulator - here's what I learned about emergent complexity

**Body:**

I just finished a Python implementation of Conway's Game of Life and wanted to share some insights about this classic cellular automaton.

**What is the Game of Life?**

It's a "zero-player game" where you set up initial conditions and watch patterns evolve. Each cell on a 2D grid is either alive (1) or dead (0), and the next state depends only on its 8 neighbors:

- **Survival:** A live cell with 2 or 3 neighbors stays alive
- **Birth:** A dead cell with exactly 3 neighbors becomes alive
- **Death:** All other cells die or stay dead

That's it—4 simple rules.

**What emerges from simplicity**

The fascinating part is what happens when you run it:

- **Still lifes** (like the "block") never change
- **Oscillators** (like the "pulsar") repeat every few generations
- **Spaceships** (like the "glider") travel across the grid
- **Methuselahs** (like the "R-pentomino") start small and explode chaotically

From my simulation: the R-pentomino starts with just 5 cells but grows to 116 cells over 100 generations, with wild population swings along the way.

**Implementation trick**

Instead of checking each cell's 8 neighbors with loops, I used scipy's `convolve2d` with a 3x3 kernel (all 1s except center 0). Much faster and cleaner!

**Why it matters**

The Game of Life is:
- Turing complete (can simulate any computation)
- A model for studying emergence in complex systems
- Used in research on self-organization and artificial life

Exploring Emergent Complexity with Conway's Game of Life

I recently implemented a computational study of Conway's Game of Life—a cellular automaton that demonstrates how complex behavior emerges from simple deterministic rules.

**Technical Approach:**
- Used NumPy and SciPy for efficient grid operations
- Implemented neighbor counting via 2D convolution (faster than iterative approaches)
- Applied periodic boundary conditions for toroidal grid topology
- Computed entropy metrics to quantify information dynamics

**Key Findings:**
The simulation revealed distinct behavioral classes from identical rule sets:
- Stable configurations (still lifes)
- Periodic oscillators (periods 2-3)
- Translating patterns (spaceships)
- Chaotic growth (methuselahs expanding from 5 to 116 cells)

**Why It Matters:**
